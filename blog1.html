🚀 How I Got into Cybersecurity and Learned Linux in Just One Week — As a Non-CS Student

Hi, I’m Raj Bharti — a passionate ethical hacking enthusiast with no formal background in Computer Science. Within just one week, I went from knowing almost nothing to confidently using Linux, writing Bash scripts, and exploring cybersecurity deeply.

💡 How I Got Interested in Cybersecurity

My curiosity started with videos like “how hackers work” and “how websites get hacked.” It soon turned into a passion. I was fascinated by:

  • How hackers think like problem-solvers
  • The logic behind vulnerabilities
  • The invisible wars happening in cyberspace

I wanted to become powerful — not to harm, but to protect and outsmart.

🐧 Why I Chose Linux First

Everyone in the cybersecurity world says: “Learn Linux. It’s the hacker’s playground.” So I challenged myself to learn it in 1 week.

📅 My 1-Week Linux Learning Journey

  • Day 1: Installed Kali Linux using VirtualBox. Faced errors, fixed them by reading forums — first hacker moment!
  • Day 2: Learned basic commands like ls, cd, rm, etc. Used NetworkChuck’s playlist.
  • Day 3–4: Wrote Bash scripts to ping websites, scan ports. Learned conditionals, loops, variables.
  • Day 5: Explored file permissions and process management with chmod, ps, kill.
  • Day 6–7: Automated tasks, learned tools like nmap, and played OverTheWire’s Bandit wargame.

🔥 What Helped Me Stay Consistent?

  • My obsession with hacking and cyber warfare
  • Watching CTFs and hacker documentaries
  • Taking daily notes
  • Not fearing the terminal — errors became my teachers

🚀 What’s Next?

  • Playing more CTF challenges (TryHackMe & Hack The Box)
  • Starting bug bounties (HackerOne, Immunefi)
  • Building my own hacking tools at rajbharti.in
  • Sharing everything I learn on this blog

🎯 Final Words

You don’t need a CS degree. You don’t need to be a genius.

  • Passion 💥
  • Patience 🧠
  • Practice 💻

If you have these, even a week can change your confidence — just like it did for me.
