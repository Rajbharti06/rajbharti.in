🤖 Why I Started Learning AI

From cybersecurity to building human-like AI projects — my journey with ChatGPT, Perplexity, and code.

When I started learning cybersecurity, I never imagined that path would lead me to AI. At first, I used tools like ChatGPT and Perplexity just to help me understand concepts and commands faster — to debug Bash scripts, explain network terms, or generate summaries from research papers. But soon, I realized AI wasn’t just a tool. It was a world of its own.

While exploring cybersecurity labs and CTFs, I started wondering how these AI models actually worked behind the scenes — how they could understand context, reason, and respond like humans. That curiosity made me start learning AI and coding side by side.

Coding wasn’t easy for me. Honestly, it felt tough. But instead of giving up, I learned to integrate my AI knowledge with code. I used AI tools to generate ideas, structure my code, and debug errors. Slowly, I began building real things — starting small, then growing into projects that mattered.

“AI became my silent teacher — available 24/7, never tired, always explaining, and always pushing me one step ahead.”

From learning to building

With every line of code I understood, I built something new. Some of my favorite projects came out of this journey:

  • My Mitra — an emotional AI mentor app built for students, completely offline, focused on privacy and connection.
  • IntervAI — an AI interview preparation platform with real-time feedback, voice input, and performance tracking.
  • Apoclyps GPT — an experimental hackathon project using LLMs to simulate crisis-solving intelligence.

These projects taught me how AI could connect deeply with humans — not just automate tasks, but understand emotions, guide people, and make learning personal.

Learning by watching and doing

I learned mostly by watching YouTube videos, trying, failing, and asking AI for help. I followed creators like Andrej Karpathy, who explained how LLMs think and how neural networks really work. His YouTube channel opened my eyes to what’s possible when math meets creativity.

I practiced prompt writing, explored open-source models, and started understanding the structure of transformers — how they learn, process tokens, and predict words. I also explored how local LLMs can run offline and be integrated into personal assistants.

Certifications and learning paths

To build credibility and structure my learning, I completed or plan to complete:

  • CS50’s Introduction to Artificial Intelligence with Python — Harvard / edX.
  • Google AI Essentials — Coursera.
  • Elements of AI — University of Helsinki.
  • AI for Everyone — Andrew Ng (Coursera).
  • IBM Generative AI Fundamentals.

What AI means to me now

AI isn’t just code to me anymore — it’s a way to express ideas. It helped me build when I didn’t know how, speak when I didn’t have confidence, and create things that matter. It taught me persistence, logical thinking, and creativity all at once. I see AI not as a replacement for humans, but as an extension of our imagination.

“I didn’t just learn AI to use it — I learned AI to understand it, and to build something human with it.”
